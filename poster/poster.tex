\begin{columns}

  \column{0.58}
  \block{Motivation}{
    \fontsize{12}{16}\selectfont
    The \emph{tubulin code} --- combinations of \(\alpha/\beta\) isotypes
    and post-translational modifications --- modulates microtubule
    composition and the interactions of tubulin with motors, MAPs and
    small molecules. The PDB now holds approximately one thousand
    tubulin-containing structures, capturing distinct conformational
    states and dozens of drug- and protein-binding pockets, while curated
    compendia such as the \tubulindb (Abbaali \emph{et al.} 2023)
    catalogue thousands of disease-linked mutations and tubulin-specific
    PTMs. Despite the wealth on both sides, the structural and
    annotation layers live in separate resources and resist
    co-exploration.

    \smallskip
    \tubexyz is a structural atlas that places every tubulin residue
    from every deposited structure into a single \emph{coordinate
    system} per family
    (\textcolor{tubAlpha}{$\alpha$},
    \textcolor{tubBeta}{$\beta$},
    \textcolor{tubGamma}{$\gamma$},
    \textcolor{tubDelta}{$\delta$},
    \textcolor{tubEpsilon}{$\varepsilon$}).
    Sequence variants, ligand-binding contacts, literature mutations and
    PTMs are programmatically lifted onto the common residue index. The
    substrate is deliberately \textbf{pluggable}: clinical variant
    catalogues, fragment screens and newly deposited structures join via
    a simple mapping with no schema changes. A 2D MSA is bidirectionally
    coupled to a Mol*-based 3D viewer; an LLM-driven chatbot translates
    plain-language questions about binding sites, cross-species residue
    comparisons, disease-linked mutations and PTMs into deep links into
    the integrated data.
  }

  \vspace{0.6em}

  % --- The big 3D render (replace placeholder with \includegraphics).
  \figplaceholder{18.5cm}{figures/showcase\_3d.png}{Mol* render of TUBB3 (chain B of PDB 6S8L, 1.80\,\AA{} X-ray). Five master positions --- 205, 262, 401, 410, 417 --- highlighted as sticks and coloured by phenotype: red for cortical dysplasia / malformations of cortical development, orange for CFEOM (extraocular fibrosis), purple for in-vitro biochemical defects. GTP and plinabulin are shown desaturated for spatial context. The R262 residue-chip overlay is captured directly inside the screenshot from expert mode.}

  \vspace{0.6em}
  % --- MSA strip
  \figplaceholder{5.0cm}{figures/showcase\_msa.png}{Nightingale MSA strip across all nine human $\beta$-isotypes (TUBB1, TUBB2A, TUBB2B, \textbf{TUBB3}, TUBB4A, TUBB4B, TUBB5, TUBB6, TUBB8). Viewport on master columns $\sim$200--420; the five disease columns are boxed. The variants aux row above the alignment shows literature-variant density per column, sourced from \tubulindb.}

  \vspace{0.8em}
  \fontsize{13}{17}\selectfont
  \textbf{What the chatbot does.} Asked \emph{``which TUBB3 mutations
  cause CFEOM and where are they on the structure?''}, the chatbot
  resolves the five master positions, opens 6S8L:B in expert mode, paints
  the residues by phenotype, and aligns the nine human $\beta$-isotype
  references in the MSA panel so the conservation pattern is obvious at a
  glance.

  \smallskip
  \textbf{What the figure shows.} All five disease columns are identical
  across every human $\beta$-isotype --- pathogenic precisely because they
  sit at residues the protein cannot afford to vary. The R$\to$C/H
  substitutions at master 262 cause congenital fibrosis of the
  extraocular muscles (CFEOM); R$\to$A at the same position is a
  biochemical kinesin-binding defect described in cell-free assays.
  Curation chain: Tischfield \emph{et al.} 2010 \emph{Cell} + Poirier
  \emph{et al.} 2010 \emph{Hum. Mol. Genet.} $\to$ TubulinDB (Abbaali
  2023) $\to$ Neo4j via Universal Tubulin Numbering.

  \block{}{
    \showcasetitle{2}{Taxane (TA1) contacts cluster on the M-loop of $\beta$-tubulin and project as an aux row onto the master alignment}
    \vspace{0.6em}

    \figplaceholder{10.5cm}{figures/ligand\_3d.png}{TA1 bound at the M-loop (S7--H9) pocket of human $\beta$-tubulin in PDB \texttt{<PDB:CHAIN>}; contact residues are coloured on the master index.}

    \vspace{0.6em}
    \figplaceholder{4.0cm}{figures/ligand\_msa.png}{Taxane contact positions painted as an aux row above the $\beta$ master alignment (TUBB1--TUBB8).}

    \vspace{0.7em}
    \fontsize{12}{15}\selectfont
    \textbf{What the chatbot does.} \emph{``Where does taxol bind in a
    human tubulin structure resolved at or below 3.5\,\AA?''} resolves
    to a faceted catalogue page filtered to human $\beta$-tubulin with
    TA1 bound, sorted by resolution; the top hit is opened in expert
    mode and the binding-site aux row is auto-toggled.

    \smallskip
    \textbf{Why this matters.} Taxane is the canonical
    microtubule-stabilising anti-cancer drug (Steinmetz \& Prota 2018);
    its contacts span H6--H7, the M-loop (S7--H9) and S9--S10. Painting
    them as an aux row exposes the contact residues across every human
    $\beta$-isotype simultaneously --- useful for predicting isotype
    selectivity and resistance-conferring positions.
  }

  \column{0.5}
  \block{}{
    \showcasetitle{3}{Phosphorylation tracks on TUBB3 reveal surface-accessible regulatory sites including $\beta$-S172}
    \vspace{0.6em}

    \figplaceholder{10.5cm}{figures/ptm\_3d.png}{Phosphorylation sites annotated on TUBB3 (6S8L:B). Surface-accessible $\beta$-S172 (CDK1 substrate; modulates GTP affinity) is highlighted.}

    \vspace{0.6em}
    \figplaceholder{4.0cm}{figures/ptm\_msa.png}{Phosphorylation aux row painted on the $\beta$ master alignment.}

    \vspace{0.7em}
    \fontsize{12}{15}\selectfont
    \textbf{What the chatbot does.} \emph{``Show me phosphorylation sites
    on human $\beta$-tubulin.''} stays on the central showcase chain
    (6S8L:B) and creates a custom aux row filtered to phosphorylation.

    \smallskip
    \textbf{Cross-species transfer.} The same paradigm carries human
    annotations onto sequences with no curated data: \emph{``... in
    \emph{T.\,gondii} tubulin --- compare sequences and highlight
    phosphorylation sites.''} aligns the host chain onto the human master
    and overlays the human-derived PTM track on top of the
    \emph{T.\,gondii} sequence in the MSA panel.
  }

\end{columns}
