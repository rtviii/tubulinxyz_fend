\begin{columns}

  \column{0.55}
  \block{Motivation}{
    The \emph{tubulin code} --- combinations of \(\alpha/\beta\) isotypes
    and post-translational modifications (PTMs) --- modulates
    microtubule composition and the interactions of tubulin with motors,
    MAPs and small molecules. The PDB now holds $\sim$1000
    tubulin-containing structures and curated compendia such as the
    \tubulindb (Abbaali \emph{et al.} 2023) catalogue thousands of
    disease-linked mutations and PTMs --- but these structural and
    annotation layers live in separate resources and resist
    co-exploration.

    \medskip
    \tubexyz places every tubulin residue from every deposited
    structure into a single \emph{coordinate system} per family
    (\textcolor{tubAlpha}{$\alpha$},
    \textcolor{tubBeta}{$\beta$},
    \textcolor{tubGamma}{$\gamma$},
    \textcolor{tubDelta}{$\delta$},
    \textcolor{tubEpsilon}{$\varepsilon$}).
    Sequence variants, ligand-binding contacts, literature mutations
    and PTMs are programmatically lifted onto the common residue index.
    The substrate is deliberately \textbf{pluggable}: new sources ---
    clinical variants, fragment screens, new structures --- join via a
    simple mapping with no schema changes. A 2D MSA coupled to a
    Mol*-based 3D viewer plus an LLM-driven chatbot facilitate
    navigation.
  }

  \column{0.45}
  \block{Database overview}{
    \input{tikz/counts_table.tex}

    \vspace{0.3em}
    {\footnotesize\textbf{Pipeline} ---
    every PDB structure is HMM-classified into a tubulin or MAP family,
    isotype-called against the human reference set, MUSCLE-aligned to a
    per-family master MSA, and persisted in Neo4j. Literature variants
    and PTMs from \tubulindb are mapped onto the same residue index via
    Universal Tubulin Numbering. New annotation sources plug in as soon
    as they carry a residue position.}
  }

\end{columns}

\block{Other queries the chatbot handles}{
  \footnotesize
  \begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.27\linewidth}@{\hspace{1em}}p{0.69\linewidth}@{}}
    \textbf{\#1 Filter + binding site} &
    \textit{``Where does taxol bind in a human tubulin structure resolved at or below 3.5\,\AA{}?''} ---
    faceted catalogue of human $\beta$-tubulin with TA1/TXL bound, sorted by resolution; taxane-site contact residues lifted onto the master alignment.
    \\[0.4em]

    \textbf{\#2 Cross-species pocket comparison} &
    \textit{``Compare the GTP binding site in human and \emph{Toxoplasma gondii} $\alpha$-tubulin.''} ---
    resolves to \texttt{6S8L:A} (human, 1.80\,\AA{}) and \texttt{9Y9Z:1A} (\emph{T.\,gondii}, 3.14\,\AA{}); 25 of $\sim$32 GTP-contact positions shared in master coords.
    \\[0.4em]

    \textbf{\#3 Annotation transfer} &
    \textit{``I'm interested in phosphorylation of \emph{T.\,gondii} tubulin, but there is data only for human --- compare sequences and highlight phosphorylation sites.''} ---
    aligns the \emph{T.\,gondii} chain to the human master, then overlays human-derived phosphorylation as a PTM track on the alignment.
    \\
  \end{tabular}
}
