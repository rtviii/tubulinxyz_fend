\begin{columns}

  \column{0.58}
  \useblockstyle{tubeBlockDivR}
  \block{Motivation}{
    \fontsize{13}{18}\selectfont
    The \emph{tubulin code} --- combinations of \(\alpha/\beta\) isotypes
    and post-translational modifications --- modulates microtubule
    composition and the interactions of tubulin with motors, MAPs and
    small molecules. The PDB now holds approximately one thousand
    tubulin-containing structures, capturing distinct conformational
    states and dozens of drug- and protein-binding pockets, while curated
    compendia such as the \tubulindb (Abbaali \emph{et al.} 2023)
    catalogue thousands of disease-linked mutations and tubulin-specific
    PTMs. Despite the wealth on both sides, the structural and
    annotation layers live in separate resources and resist
    co-exploration.

    \par\vspace{0.5em}
    \tubexyz is a structural atlas that places every tubulin residue
    from every deposited structure into a single \emph{coordinate
    system} per family
    (\textcolor{tubAlpha}{$\alpha$},
    \textcolor{tubBeta}{$\beta$},
    \textcolor{tubGamma}{$\gamma$},
    \textcolor{tubDelta}{$\delta$},
    \textcolor{tubEpsilon}{$\varepsilon$}).
    Sequence variants, ligand-binding contacts, literature mutations and
    PTMs are programmatically lifted onto the common residue index. The
    substrate is deliberately \textbf{pluggable}: clinical variant
    catalogues, fragment screens and newly deposited structures join via
    a simple mapping with no schema changes.

    \par\vspace{0.5em}
    A 2D MSA is bidirectionally coupled to a Mol*-based 3D viewer; an
    LLM-driven chatbot translates plain-language questions about binding
    sites, cross-species residue comparisons, disease-linked mutations
    and PTMs into deep links into the integrated data.
  }

  \vspace{0.8em}
  \fontsize{13}{17}\selectfont
  Five disease-linked TUBB3 mutations --- clinical phenotypes CFEOM
  (extraocular fibrosis) and cortical dysplasia, plus an in-vitro
  kinesin-binding defect --- sit at residues conserved across all nine
  human $\beta$-isotypes. Their pathogenicity reflects positions the
  protein cannot afford to vary; lifted onto the family master
  alignment, the same coordinate exposes structural and literature
  variant layers simultaneously. Curation chain: Tischfield
  \emph{et al.} 2010 \emph{Cell} + Poirier \emph{et al.} 2010
  \emph{Hum. Mol. Genet.} $\to$ TubulinDB (Abbaali 2023) $\to$ Neo4j
  via Universal Tubulin Numbering.

    \vspace{0.7em}
    \fontsize{12}{15}\selectfont
    The taxane-stabilising anti-cancer drug paclitaxel contacts the
    M-loop (S7--H9) of $\beta$-tubulin, with additional contacts
    spanning H6--H7 and S9--S10 (Steinmetz \& Prota 2018). Projecting
    binding contacts onto the master alignment exposes the contact
    residues across every human $\beta$-isotype simultaneously,
    revealing positions where isotype-specific substitutions may
    underlie differential drug response and resistance.
  }

    \vspace{0.6em}

    \vspace{0.7em}
    \fontsize{12}{15}\selectfont
    Annotated phosphorylation sites on TUBB3 include the regulatory
    residue $\beta$-S172, a CDK1 substrate whose modification modulates
    GTP affinity and microtubule dynamics. Mapping all phosphorylation
    annotations onto the master alignment exposes which residues are
    uniformly phosphorylatable across $\beta$-isotypes and which are
    isotype-specific --- and supports annotation transfer onto
    sequences from species lacking curated PTM data.
  }
